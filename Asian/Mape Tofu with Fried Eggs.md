Mape Tofu with Fried Eggs  
  
Serves 4-6  
250g basmati rice  
500g mushrooms, roughly chopped  
110g jar of chiu chow chilli (chilli flakes in oil)   
50g ginger, finely grated  
50g garlic, minced  
3 tbsp tomato puree  
1 tsp cloves, toasted and ground   
1 tsp cumin, toasted and ground   
3 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, toasted and ground   
1 tsp soy sauce  
4-8 eggs   
300g silken or soft tofu, cubed  
4 spring onions, thinly sliced, to serve  
1 tsp salt and 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, ground together  
1 tsp salt and 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, ground together  
  
1 Start by cooking the rice, preferably using a cast-iron pan with a tight-fitting lid. Rinse the rice until the water runs clear, then put in pan and cover with about 15mm of fresh water and a pinch of salt. Bring to the boil over the biggest ring on your hob. Once boiling, transfer to the smallest ring on the lowest heat and cook with the lid on for 15-20 minutes. (There’s plenty of time to get the rest of the recipe done in the meantime.) Resist the urge to lift the lid before that time, because you will lose all the steam. Once cooked, mix the rice around a bit with a fork. Keep it warm for serving.  
  
2 Saute the mushrooms with a touch of oil over a high heat in a well-seasoned or nonstick frying pan till you’ve got a decent amount of colour. Turn off the heat and set aside.  
2 Saute the mushrooms with a touch of oil over a high heat in a well-seasoned or nonstick frying pan till you’ve got a decent amount of colour. Turn off the heat and set aside.  
  
3 Add 1 tbsp or so of the oil from the chiu chow chilli jar to a pan big enough to accommodate all the ingredients, then warm over a low-medium heat. Once the oil has heated a little, sweat the ginger for a minute or two, then add the garlic and continue to cook for a further minute or so. Dig out 2 tbsp of the chiu chow chilli and mix it into the ginger and garlic.

6 Spoon a mound of steaming rice followed by the sauce into individual bowls. Pop the tofu on to the sauce and top with the eggs. The heat from the sauce below and the egg above will heat the tofu sufficiently. Scatter the spring onion over the eggs and sprinkle with the Sichuan salt.
