# Almond Paste Scones (WSJ)

- TOTAL TIME: 1 hour
- MAKES: 8 scones
- For the scones:
- 2 cups (280 grams) all — purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon (14 grams) baking powder
- 3 tablespoons (38 grams) sugar
- ½ teaspoon (2 grams) kosher salt
- 5 tablespoons (70 grams) cold unsalted butter, cut into 1/4 — inch cubes
- 1 cup (227 ml) heavy cream
- For the almond paste:
- Heaping ¾ cup (90 grams) almond flour
- 9 tablespoons (70 grams) powdered sugar
- 1½ tablespoons (20 ml) water
- ½ teaspoon (2.5 ml) almond extract
- For assembly:
- 3 teaspoons raspberry jam (optional)
- Granulated or turbinado sugar, to sprinkle (optional)

1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees. In a mixing bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, sugar and salt. Use your fingers to rub cold, cubed butter into dry ingredients until powdery with some pea-size chunks of butter. Pour in cream, reserving 1 tablespoon for brushing, and stir to form a soft dough. (Try not to overmix.) Gently knead in any stray crumbs. Wrap dough in plastic or cover bowl with a towel. Transfer to refrigerator to rest while you make almond paste.
2. <br />
3. In a food processor, combine almond flour and powdered sugar, and process to combine. Add water and almond extract, and process to form a paste.
4. <br />
5. On a floured surface, roll out dough into a 6-by-12-inch rectangle. Roll out almond paste into a long rectangle around the same width but twice the length of the dough. Cut paste into 4 squares.
6. <br />
7. Place one square of almond paste on bottom of dough rectangle. Spread 1 teaspoon jam, if using, over almond paste. Fold top piece of dough over bottom and roll out dough to 6 by 12 inches. Repeat with almond paste (and jam, if using) 3 times.
8. <br />
9. Cut dough in half, then in quarters, to make 8 scones. Brush scones with reserved cream. Sprinkle with sugar, if using. Transfer scones to a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake until golden and fragrant, 25-28 minutes.
