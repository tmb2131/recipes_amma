Basic Short-Crust Pastry (NYT)  
  
For quiche I recommend short-crust pastry (as opposed to flaky). Made with all-purpose flour, butter, salt and ice water, it’s perfect for a savory tart, and it takes literally minutes to make. A mixer or food processor can produce a very good dough, but even if hand-mixed, it goes fast. The key is to keep the butter cold, and to blend it quickly with the flour until it resembles coarse meal. Once the water is added, work the mixture only enough to form a soft dough ball. Flatten the ball to a thick disk, wrap and refrigerate for several hours before rolling. An overnight rest in the fridge is ideal. Let it come to cool room temperature before rolling.  
Roll it out gradually, allowing it a chance to rest for a moment before continuing. This makes rolling easier, and guarantees the dough won’t shrink in the pan during pre-baking. Pre-baking the shell, also called blind baking, ensures the pastry won’t go soggy from the filling. If you are lining a 9 ½-inch tart pan, you will need a 12-inch circle of dough. Lay the dough loosely into the pan, letting it relax a bit. Fold the overlap around the perimeter to make a double thickness, then press firmly against the pan so the finished edge is slightly higher than the pan. If possible, refrigerate or freeze the pressed-in dough for an hour before pre-baking.  
Bake for 10 to 15 minutes at 375 degrees, until lightly browned. Some cooks like to use pie weights for blind baking, but a poke with a wooden spoon is the method I prefer. Check the tart shell frequently during the first few minutes. If the bottom is puffing up, push down with the spoon to deflate. Should the edges begin to flop down, press them back with the spoon. Cool the pre-baked shell completely (you can pre-bake it several hours ahead if you wish).  
  
  
  
One 9 and ½-inch tart crust  
145 grams all-purpose flour (about 1 cup)  
145 grams all-purpose flour (about 1 cup)  
½ teaspoon kosher salt  
1 stick cold unsalted butter (¼ pound), cut in ⅛-inch pieces  
1 stick cold unsalted butter (¼ pound), cut in ⅛-inch pieces  
3 tablespoons ice water  
  
 Put flour and salt in the bowl of a stand mixer or food processor. Add butter and quickly cut it into flour until mixture resembles coarse meal.  
 Add ice water and mix briefly, about 30 seconds, to form a soft dough. Remove dough, shape into a thick disk, wrap in plastic and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Bring to cool room temperature before rolling.  
 To roll, lightly flour dough and counter. Roll out gradually, periodically letting dough rest for a moment before continuing. This makes rolling easier and will keep dough from shrinking back during baking.  
 Roll dough to a thin round approximately 13 inches in diameter, then trim to make a 12-inch circle (refrigerate and save trimmings for patching). Lay dough loosely into a 9½-inch fluted tart pan with removable bottom, letting it relax a bit. Fold overlap back inside to make a double thickness, then press firmly against the pan so the finished edge is slightly higher than the pan. Refrigerate or freeze for an hour before pre-baking.
