## Blackberry, Apple, Marzipan Crumble  
Adapted from “Greenfeast” by Nigel Slater  
Adapted from “Greenfeast” by Nigel Slater  
  
TOTAL TIME: 1¼ hours SERVES: 6  
For the filling:  
2 pounds tart apples, such as Granny Smith  
1½ tablespoons butter  
2 tablespoons sugar  
⅓ cup apple juice  
¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon  
1 pint blackberries  
For the crumble:  
6 tablespoons butter  
1¼ cups all-purpose flour  
5 tablespoons ground almonds or almond flour  
3½ ounces almond paste or marzipan paste  
½ cup almond slices  
1 cup heavy cream, for serving  
1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.  
2. Make the filling: Peel, quarter and core apples. Cut each quarter into two segments. In a shallow pan over moderate heat, melt butter. Add apples and sugar and cook, turning occasionally, until they turn a pale gold, about 5 minutes. Stir in apple juice, cinnamon and blackberries. Transfer to a deep 8- or 9-inch square baking dish.  
3. Make the crumble: Use your fingertips or a food processor to rub butter into flour until you have fine crumbs, then fold in ground almonds. Tear marzipan into shards about 1 inch in size, and stir into crumble without blending.  
4. Loosely scatter crumble over fruit, then flaked almonds. Bake until crumble is golden, 50-60 minutes. Set aside to cool for 10 minutes before serving. Serve warm, passing a pitcher of heavy cream.
