# Kate Jones_ La Colombe Olive-Oil Cake (WSJ)

- Scant 1 cup sugar
- Zest of 1 lime
- Heaping ⅓ teaspoon ground cloves
- Heaping ⅓ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- Heaping ¾ teaspoon baking soda
- Heaping ¾ teaspoon baking powder
- 2 cups all — purpose flour
- Heaping ½ teaspoon salt
- 3 large eggs
- 1¼ cups whole milk
- ½ cup fresh orange juice
- 1¼ — 1½ cups extra — virgin olive oil

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Grease a large loaf pan (13 by 4 by 4 inches) with butter.
2. <br />
3. In a large bowl, whisk together sugar, lime zest and spices, and set aside. The longer the mix sits, the more the citrus will infuse the sugar. In a medium bowl, whisk together baking soda, baking powder, flour and salt.
4. <br />
5. Whisk eggs into spiced sugar very thoroughly. Whisk in milk and orange juice. Add olive oil gradually, whisking continuously. Gradually whisk dry mixture into wet batter until just combined.
6. <br />
7. Pour batter into prepared pan and bake for about 1 hour. Baking time will vary depending on how much olive oil is in the batter. Start checking at 50 minutes and continue every 5-10 minutes, until the cake’s center springs back when you press it with your thumb. To serve, cut into slices 1 inch thick.
