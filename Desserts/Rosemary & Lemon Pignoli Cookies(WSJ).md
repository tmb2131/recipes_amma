**Rosemary & Lemon Pignoli Cookies(WSJ)**  
**Rosemary & Lemon Pignoli Cookies(WSJ)**  
The almond paste called for in these cookies is usually sold in 7- or 8-ounce tubes or blocks in the baking aisle.  
  
  
TOTAL TIME: 1 hour 45 minutes  
TOTAL TIME: 1 hour 45 minutes  
10-12 cookies  
  
Ingredients  
* 		1½ teaspoons dried rosemary  
* 		½ cup plus ¾ cup pine nuts  
* 		14 ounces almond paste, cut into 1-inch chunks (about 2 cups)  
* 		1 cup sugar  
* 		2 large egg whites  
* 		1½ tablespoons packed grated lemon zest (from about 2 lemons)  
* 		1 teaspoon kosher salt  
Directions  
1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and spray with nonstick cooking spray.  
2. Use a spice grinder or mortar and pestle to grind dried rosemary into a coarse powder. In a food processor, blend ½ cup pine nuts to form a paste, about 1 minute. Add almond paste and process until well combined.  
3. Add sugar and pulse to combine. Add egg whites, lemon zest, salt and ground rosemary, and process to form a smooth dough. (At this point, the dough can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, or frozen for up to 2 weeks. Thaw completely before rolling.)  
4. Place remaining ¾ cup pine nuts in a small bowl. Roll dough into 1½-inch balls (about 2 tablespoons each) and press top of each ball into pine nuts.  
5. Place remaining ¾ cup pine nuts in a small bowl. Roll dough into 1½-inch balls (about 2 tablespoons each) and press top of each ball into pine nuts.  
6. Arrange cookies, pine nut–side up, on prepared baking sheet, spaced about 2 inches apart. With the tips of your fingers, gently flatten balls into discs about ¾ inch thick. Bake, rotating pan from front to back halfway through, until golden brown, about 20 minutes.  
7. Let cool before serving. Leftovers will keep, in an airtight container at room temperature, for up to 4 days.
