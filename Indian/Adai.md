# Adai

- 0.5 cup ponni rice
- 1 cup idli rice
- 0.5 cup tuvar dal
- 0.5 cup chana dal
- 0.25 cup urad dal
- Soak above for 2 hours (soak rice and dal separately)
- Grind with,
- Ginger
- Red chillies 2 or 3
- Asafoedita
- Curry leaves
- Salt

The batter should be coarse and thick (of pourable consistency, though).
Spread a ladleful of the batter on the griddle. Make a hole in the middle and pour 1 tsp of oil in it and around the adai.  Cook on medium heat.  It will take a while to cook.  Flip over and add 0.5 tsp of oil to brown the other side.
Serve hot with a little nei on top, and maple syrup or vellam on the side.

Remember to have yoghurt.

You can add grated coconut and or very finely chopped onion to the batter.
Caution: the batter will spoil if you store it with the additions.

Set aside any batter you plan to store and use through coconut/onion only with the batter you plan to use.
