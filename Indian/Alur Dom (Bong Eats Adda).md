1. Baby potatoes \- best with niblet potatoes. But big potatoes cut into small pieces never worked for me at all. (I used 1.5 lbs)  
2. Sada jeera seeds and powder (need both)  
3. Whole shukno lonka and powder (need both)  
4. Tomatoes (3 big ones)  
5. Vegetable oil \- try to avoid mustard oil.   
6. Bhaja moshla

Also, salt and sugar to taste.  
optional- dhoney pata, tetul jol, kacha lonka kuchi.  
Method:  
Boil the potatoes with sufficient salt till they are done. Peel them.  
Sprinkle salt (again) and red chilli powder (generously- heat is one of the main flavor for this snack) on them (you can add a couple of pinches of turmeric just for the color) and fry them in vegetable oil till they firm up and have a nice brown coating. Make sure they don’t break.  
Take them off and add whole dry red chilli (shukno lonka) to the oil. Finely chop 3 big tomatoes like this.   
Once the red chillis start to smoke, add the jeera seeds.  
Then add the chopped tomatoes and about 2-3 teaspoons of jeera powder and cook them till the tomatoes melt and turn into a slurry. Add salt to taste and sugar as well (it balances the acidity from the tomatoes).  
Go ahead and add the potatoes back and cook for sometime till the water released by the tomatoes dry out quite a bit. Mince some fresh green chillis and sprinkle over. Add the bhaja moshla now or you can sprinkle it over while serving.   
The cooking is essentially done. But at this point if you add some chopped coriander leaves and tetul jol \- it starts to taste a bit like phuchkawala’s alur dom (this part was our addition). If you don’t add this part, you can squeeze some a lemon on it before serving.
