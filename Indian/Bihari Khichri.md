# Bihari Khichri

- Raw Rice — 1 cup
- Split Yellow Moong dal — 1/2 cup
- Whole Masoor dal — 1/2 cup
- Mixed vegetables — 1 cup (I used beans, carrot, and potatoes)
- Oil — 2 tbsp
- Ghee — 2 tbsp
- Dry red chillies 1 no
- Bay leaves — 2 nos
- Cumin seeds — 1 tsp
- Ginger garlic paste — 1 tsp
- Turmeric 0.75 tsp
- Salt to taste
- To roast and powder:
- Cardamom — 1
- Cloves — 3
- Black Cardmom — 1
- Cinnamon stick — 1 inch
- Chilli powder — 1/2 tsp
- Cumin seeds — 1 tsp
- Coriander seeds — 2 tsp

Wash and soak the rice.
Roast the moong dal in a dry skillet until you get a faint aroma.( Do NOT over roast). Remove and soak with masoor dal for 5 minutes and then strain.

Strain the rice and keep separate.
Heat 2 tbsp ghee in a large heavy pot, add the dry red chilli, bay leaves, cumin seeds, and then the potatoes.

When they colour slightly add ginger garlic paste and turmeric & fry for a few minutes.
Add now the masoor dal, moong dal. Fry for a minute or two.
Add the rice and saute for few minutes,  add remaining vegetables, 3 cups of water, bring to a boil and allow to simmer covered, on a medium-low flame. Check every now and then adding water if necessary. Add salt when almost done.

Meanwhile roast all the spices given, under the list to Grind and grind as a fine powder.
Once khichri is done add the ground spice powder, ghee and mix well.
