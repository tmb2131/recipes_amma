# Carrot Halwa

- ACTIVE TIME: 15 minutes
- TOTAL TIME: 1¼ hours
- SERVES: 4
- For the halwa:
- 16 medium carrots, washed and peeled
- 3½ cups whole milk
- 3 tablespoons ghee
- ⅓ cup sugar
- ½ teaspoon freshly ground green cardamom
- For the topping:
- 1 tablespoon ghee
- 2 tablespoons chopped almonds
- 2 tablespoons chopped pistachios
- 2 tablespoons chopped cashews
- 3 tablespoons golden raisins
- ¼ teaspoon ground green cardamom

Make the halwa: Grate carrots. Combine carrots and milk in a saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and gently simmer, stirring occasionally, 1 hour. Stir in ghee, sugar and cardamom. Continue cooking until milk has nearly entirely evaporated or been absorbed by the carrots, 15 minutes more. The carrots should be meltingly tender. If not, add a splash more milk and cook 10 minutes more. (At this point, the halwa is traditionally served, but you can also give it a quick blitz in a food processor if you’d like a smoother pudding. If you do this, transfer the purée back to the pot to warm it before serving.)

Make the topping: Warm ghee in a frying pan over medium heat. Add nuts and toast until fragrant, about 3 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in raisins.

Stir topping into carrot halwa and cook 5 minutes more. Alternatively, transfer carrot halwa to a serving bowl and sprinkle with the topping. Halwa may be eaten hot or cold. Just before serving, give it a light dusting of ground cardamom.
