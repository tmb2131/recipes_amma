# Hotel Sambar 3

- Moong dal 0.25 cup
- Tuvar dal 0.25 cup
- Instant sambar powder 1 tbsp
- Turmeric powder 0.5 tsp
- Cayenne 0.5 tsp
- Coriander powder 1.5+ tsp
- Asafoetida pinch
- Salt to taste
- Besan flour 1 tbsp
- Mustard seeds
- Cumin seeds
- Dry chilli 1 whole
- Curry leaves
- Corriander

Pressure cook the dals with carrots, whole green chillies and haldi.

Cook the murungakkai and whole tomatoes in a plate on top of the dals.

Heat a large sauce pan with 1 tbsp oil.
Fry the shallots.
Add instant sambar powder, coriander powder, turmeric, cayenne and asafoetida.
Pour in the tamarind juice and water. Bring to boil and simmer for 8 to 10 minutes.

Add the cooked dal and veggies.
Simmer for a few minutes longer.
Heat 2 tbsp oil in a small frying pan.  Lower heat and add besan and stir quickly as it froths up.  ***Be careful***.
In seconds it will smell nutty.  Pour it into the sambar and stir well. (To ensure that it doesn't lump, you can mix it into half a cup hot water and pour that into the sambar).

Simmer and switch stove off.
Heat 2 tsp oil.  Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, chilli & asafoetida. Add a few curry leaves. Pour over sambar.
Garnish with curry & coriander leaves.
