# Keralan curry paste and garam masala & Punjabi Curry sauce (BBC)

- 65g/2¼oz [**desiccated coconut**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/dessicated_coconut) soaked in 100ml/3½fl oz hot water: 65g/2¼oz desiccated coconut soaked in 100ml/3½fl oz hot water
- 40g/1½ oz fresh root [**ginger**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/ginger), roughly chopped : 40g/1½ oz fresh root ginger, roughly chopped
- 1 tsp ground [**turmeric**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/turmeric): 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 2 tsp ground [**cumin**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cumin), dry toasted in a pan : 2 tsp ground cumin, dry toasted in a pan
- 100ml/3½fl oz [**coconut milk**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/coconut_milk): 100ml/3½fl oz coconut milk
- 1 tsp [**salt**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/salt): 1 tsp salt
- 30ml [**sunflower oil**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/sunflower_oil): 30ml sunflower oil
- 1 tsp black [**mustard seeds**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/mustard_seeds): 1 tsp black mustard seeds
- 10 — 12 fresh [**curry leaves**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/curry_leaves): 10 — 12 fresh curry leaves
- 2 — 3 whole red [**chillies**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/chilli) (kashmiri, kanthari or bird's eye will work): 2 — 3 whole red chillies (kashmiri, kanthari or bird's eye will work)
- For the garam masala
- 1 tsp [**peppercorns**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/peppercorn): 1 tsp peppercorns
- 6 green [**cardamom**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cardamom) pods: 6 green cardamom pods
- 2 [**star anise**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/star_anise): 2 star anise
- 5 [**cloves**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cloves): 5 cloves
- 5cm/2in [**cinnamon**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cinnamon) stick : 5cm/2in cinnamon stick
- 2 tsp [**fennel seeds**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/fennel_seeds): 2 tsp fennel seeds
- 2 tbsp [**coriander seeds**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/coriander_seeds): 2 tbsp coriander seeds
- ¼ [**mace**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/mace) blade : ¼ mace blade
- ½ tsp ground [**nutmeg**](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/nutmeg): ½ tsp ground nutmeg

<br />

1. To make the paste, put the desiccated coconut (and soaking water), ginger, turmeric, cumin, coconut milk and salt into a blender and blend to a fine paste. Add little drops of water to make a creamy consistency, if necessary. Set aside.

2. Heat the oil in a frying pan. Once hot, add the mustard seeds, curry leaves and whole chillies, breaking the chillies up with a wooden spoon. As soon as they start to sizzle, add the prepared paste and mix well. Cook for 4–5 minutes over a medium heat. Once done, allow to cool before transferring to a sterilised jar. Will keep in the fridge for up to one week, or you can freeze it.

3. To make the garam masala (which I add with the paste to meat dishes - see Recipe Tips), tip the ingredients into a small frying pan over a low heat. Lightly dry-roast until aromatic, then remove from the heat and allow to cool. Once cool, grind to a powder consistency.

Punjabi Curry Sauce
**Ingredients**

* 50ml/2fl oz **[rapeseed oil](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/rapeseed_oil)**: 50ml/2fl oz rapeseed oil

* 1 large **[garlic](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/garlic)** bulb, cloves finely chopped : 1 large garlic bulb, cloves finely chopped

* 50g/1¾oz fresh root **[ginger](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/ginger)**, finely chopped : 50g/1¾oz fresh root ginger, finely chopped

* 2 large **[red onions](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/red_onion)**, finely chopped : 2 large red onions, finely chopped

* 2 large **[tomatoes](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/tomato)**, roughly chopped: 2 large tomatoes, roughly chopped

* 4–5 bird’s-eye **[chillies](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/chilli)**, chopped: 4–5 bird’s-eye chillies, chopped

* small handful **[fresh coriander](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/coriander_fresh)**, chopped : small handful fresh coriander, chopped

* 2 tsp **[salt](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/salt)**: 2 tsp salt

* 1 tsp chilli flakes or **[chilli powder](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/chilli_powder)**: 1 tsp chilli flakes or chilli powder

* 1 heaped tsp ground **[turmeric](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/turmeric)**: 1 heaped tsp ground turmeric

* 2 heaped tsp **[garam masala](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/garam_masala)**: 2 heaped tsp garam masala

* sprinkle dried **[fenugreek](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/fenugreek)** leaves: sprinkle dried fenugreek leaves

**Method**

1. Heat a lidded heavy-based pan over a high heat. Pour in the oil and, once hot, add the garlic and ginger and cook for 2 minutes. Add the onion, stir, then place the lid on the pan. Continue to cook over a high heat for 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally.

2. Add the tomatoes and chillies. Give everything a good stir and cook for 3 minutes with the lid on. Add the coriander and cook for 2 minutes. Add the salt and all the spices. Stir well for about 30 seconds, then remove from the heat and allow to cool.

3. Once cool, place the mixture into a blender with 2 tablespoons of water and blend to a fine paste. See Recipe Tips for how to make it into a delicious curry. You can store the paste in the fridge for up to 3 days, or freeze for up to 3 months.
