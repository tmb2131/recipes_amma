# Keralan curry paste and garam masala & Punjabi Curry sauce (BBC)

- See Method section

***

**For the paste**

* 65g/2¼oz **[desiccated coconut](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/dessicated_coconut)** soaked in 100ml/3½fl oz hot water
* 40g/1½ oz fresh root **[ginger](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/ginger)**, roughly chopped
* 1 tsp ground **[turmeric](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/turmeric)**
* 2 tsp ground **[cumin](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cumin)**, dry toasted in a pan
* 100ml/3½fl oz **[coconut milk](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/coconut_milk)**
* 1 tsp **[salt](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/salt)**
* 30ml **[sunflower oil](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/sunflower_oil)**
* 1 tsp black **[mustard seeds](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/mustard_seeds)**
* 10–12 fresh **[curry leaves](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/curry_leaves)**
* 2–3 whole red **[chillies](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/chilli)** (kashmiri, kanthari or bird's eye will work)

**For the garam masala**

* 1 tsp **[peppercorns](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/peppercorn)**
* 6 green **[cardamom](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cardamom)** pods
* 2 **[star anise](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/star_anise)**
* 5 **[cloves](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cloves)**
* 5cm/2in **[cinnamon](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/cinnamon)** stick
* 2 tsp **[fennel seeds](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/fennel_seeds)**
* 2 tbsp **[coriander seeds](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/coriander_seeds)**
* ¼ **[mace](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/mace)** blade
* ½ tsp ground **[nutmeg](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/nutmeg)**

<br />

**Method**

1. To make the paste, put the desiccated coconut (and soaking water), ginger, turmeric, cumin, coconut milk and salt into a blender and blend to a fine paste. Add little drops of water to make a creamy consistency, if necessary. Set aside. 
2. Heat the oil in a frying pan. Once hot, add the mustard seeds, curry leaves and whole chillies, breaking the chillies up with a wooden spoon. As soon as they start to sizzle, add the prepared paste and mix well. Cook for 4–5 minutes over a medium heat. Once done, allow to cool before transferring to a sterilised jar. Will keep in the fridge for up to one week, or you can freeze it.
3. To make the garam masala (which I add with the paste to meat dishes - see Recipe Tips), tip the ingredients into a small frying pan over a low heat. Lightly dry-roast until aromatic, then remove from the heat and allow to cool. Once cool, grind to a powder consistency.

Punjabi Curry Sauce

**Ingredients**

<br />

* 50ml/2fl oz **[rapeseed oil](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/rapeseed_oil)**
* 1 large **[garlic](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/garlic)** bulb, cloves finely chopped
* 50g/1¾oz fresh root **[ginger](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/ginger)**, finely chopped
* 2 large **[red onions](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/red_onion)**, finely chopped
* 2 large **[tomatoes](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/tomato)**, roughly chopped
* 4–5 bird’s-eye **[chillies](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/chilli)**, chopped
* small handful **[fresh coriander](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/coriander_fresh)**, chopped
* 2 tsp **[salt](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/salt)**
* 1 tsp chilli flakes or **[chilli powder](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/chilli_powder)**
* 1 heaped tsp ground **[turmeric](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/turmeric)**
* 2 heaped tsp **[garam masala](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/garam_masala)**
* sprinkle dried **[fenugreek](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/fenugreek)** leaves

<br />

**Method**

1. Heat a lidded heavy-based pan over a high heat. Pour in the oil and, once hot, add the garlic and ginger and cook for 2 minutes. Add the onion, stir, then place the lid on the pan. Continue to cook over a high heat for 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally. 
2. Add the tomatoes and chillies. Give everything a good stir and cook for 3 minutes with the lid on. Add the coriander and cook for 2 minutes. Add the salt and all the spices. Stir well for about 30 seconds, then remove from the heat and allow to cool. 
3. Once cool, place the mixture into a blender with 2 tablespoons of water and blend to a fine paste. See Recipe Tips for how to make it into a delicious curry. You can store the paste in the fridge for up to 3 days, or freeze for up to 3 months.
