# Kurma 1 & 2

- See Method section

***

Veg kurma

**Prepare vegetables – Chop the following:**

2 medium onions (1 cup finely chopped)\
1 tbsp ginger garlic paste\
2 medium tomatoes (¾ cup fine chopped)\
1 medium carrot (¾ cup, half inch pieces)\
2 medium potatoes (1 cup, half inch pieces)\
¾ cup green peas\
8 to 10 French beans (¾ cup, half inch pieces)\
¾ to 1 cup cauliflower florets

<br />

**Kurma paste: To a grinder jar, add**

¼ cup grated coconut (or ¼ cup coconut milk)\
1 teaspoon fennel seeds (skip if you don’t like)\
1 tablespoon roasted gram (optional)\
12 cashew nuts (or 12 soaked almonds or 2 tbsps poppy seeds)\
Pour 6 tablespoons water and make a fine paste. We don’t want a coarse paste.

<br />

**Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a pot. Add**

1 bay leaf\
2 green cardamoms\
½ teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera)\
3 cloves\
1 inch cinnamon piece\
1 star anise. You can also add a single strand of mace If you like the fragrance.

<br />

Next add 1 cup very finely chopped onions & 1 slit green chili (optional). Saute until the onions turn pink. The raw smell of the onions has to go away.

Add ¾ to 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste. My ginger garlic paste has turmeric in it, so the color. Saute for 1 to 2 mins, until the raw smell of the ginger garlic has gone away.

Then add ¾ cup chopped tomatoes and salt.

Fry until they turn mushy.

Add chilli powder, garam masala and cilantro. 

Saute until mixture begins to pull away from sides.

Add ground masala. Mix. Simmer on low heat for a minute or two.

Add vegetables. Mix.

Add 1.5 cups of water and salt.  Bring to a simmer. Cover and cook until vegetables are done.

***

**Ingredients**

4 turnips ( chopped)\
1 onion (chopped)\
1 tomato (chopped)\
1 &  1/2  cups of coconut milk\
2  tsp chilli powder\
1/2 tsp turmeric powder\
1 tbsp oil\
Salt to taste

MASALA  ITEMS TO TEMPER\
1/2 tsp saunf\
2 star anise\
2 small cinnamon stick\
1 bay leaf\
3 cloves

TO GRIND

5 cashews\
2 inch long piece of ginger\
5 cloves garlic\
1/2 tsp saunf

<br />

**METHOD**

Wash and peel the skin of the turnips,chop them and keep it aside.

Grind the items under ‘to grind’ column

Heat oil in a pan, add all the masala items and saute for a minute.

<br />

Add onions and saute for 2 mins on medium flame then add the tomatoes and saute for 3 mins on medium flame.

Add turnips and saute for 3-5 mins on medium flame,stir occasionally.Now add the ground mixture to the sauteed veggies.

<br />

Add the coconut milk,turmeric powder,chilli powder,salt and bring it to boil.Stir well so that the mixture spreads all through the veggies.

<br />

Transfer this to a cooker and cook on high flame for 2 whistles,then reduce to low flame and cook for 5-8 minsmins.Open the lid when the pressure is released completely.Let the kurma stay on low flame for 5 more mins without the lid so that the oil separates and the kurma becomes thick.
