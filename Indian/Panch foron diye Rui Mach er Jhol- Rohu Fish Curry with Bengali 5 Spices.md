# Panch foron diye Rui Mach er Jhol- Rohu Fish Curry with Bengali 5 Spices

- Rui/Rohu Fish — 6 pieces
- Potato — 1 big sized
- Jhinge/Ridge gourd — 1 medium sized
- Potol/Pointed Gourd — 2 — 3
- Tomato —  — 2 (finely chopped)
- Slit green chillies — 8 — 9
- Cumin powder — 1 tbsp
- Coriander powder — 1 tsp
- Turmeric powder
- Ginger paste — 1 tsp
- Salt
- Mustard oil
- Freshly chopped coriander leaves — 1 tbsp
- For tempering:
- Panch phoron (Bengali 5 Spice) — 3/4 tsp

***

Peel and cut the potato into halves lengthwise, then cut each half lengthwise into 3 pieces. Wash and keep aside.

Scrap the skin of the ridge gourd slightly to remove the hard areas. Cut the ridge gourd into tubes and half the tubes vertically. Wash and keep aside.

Cut the ends of the potol and peel skin in strips, giving the potol a stripped effect. Cut into halves lengthwise, then cut each half lengthwise into 2 pieces.

Rub salt and 1/2 tsp of turmeric powder on the freshly washed pieces of Rui/Rohu. Heat 1/2 cup oil in a kadai/deep frying pan and shallow fry the fish pieces. Remove and keep aside.


In the remaining oil shallow fry all the veggies until light brown in colour. Remove and keep aside. 

There should be 3 tbsp oil in the kadai, if not, add some more oil at this stage. For tempering add panch foron. Once it splutters add the chopped tomatoes, salt, cumin powder, coriander powder, slit green chillies and 1/2 tsp of turmeric powder. Mix and fry this on medium heat till the masala starts leaving oil. With the help of the spatula mash the tomatoes. Now add 2 cup of water. Mix and give this a quick boil on high heat. Reduce the heat, add fish pieces and all the veggies. Cover and cook on medium heat until the veggies are done. The curry will be soupy not thick. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Remove from heat.
