# Punjabi Kala Chana Masala

- Black Chickpeas/Kala Chana — 1 Cup
- Bay Leaf — 1
- Cinnamon — 1" pieces
- Clove — 1
- Green Cardamom — 1
- Onion — 2 large
- Tomato — 2 Large
- Green Chilli — 2
- Ginger — Garlic Paste — 2 tsp
- Fennel Seeds — 1/2 tsp
- Cumin Seeds — 1/2 tsp
- Red Chilli — 1 1/2 tsp
- Coriander Seeds — 1 1/2 tsp
- Turmeric Powder — 1/2 tsp
- Punjabi Chole Masala — 1 tsp (or garam masala)
- Dry Mango Powder/Amchur Powder — 1/2 tsp
- Oil — 3 tbsp
- Hing — 1/4 tsp
- Salt — To Taste

Wash and soak kala channa over night, next day rinse 3-4 times in water and pressure cook with little salt, bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom ,clove and water for 3-4 whistles. Once pressure released discard whole gram masala.

Cut one onion in large chunks and another chop finely and chop tomatoes.

Heat 1/2 tbsp of oil a wok and saute onion chunks until light brown, allow to cool and grind with green chili, cumin seeds, fennel seeds with little water into fine paste. Puree tomato and keep aside.

In a same wok heat remaining oil, add hing and chopped onion, ginger-garlic paste and saute until golden brown.

Now add onion paste and saute until oil starts to separate. Add chilli, coriander seeds, turmeric powders and mix well, saute for a minute.

Add puree tomato and mix well, cook in medium to slow until oil separates. Add cooked chana along with water, salt and bring it to boil.

Add chole masala or garam masala, amchur powder and simmer for 10 minutes or until gravy thick.
Garnish with cilantro and serve.  As you can see we had it with rice along with some salad on side.
