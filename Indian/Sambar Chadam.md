**Ingredients**

* 1 cup Rice  
*  1 cup tuvar dal  
* 1.5 teaspoon tamarind paste or large marble sized piece of tamarind soaked in 0.5 cup water   
* Cubed squash or carrots   
* Mixed vegetables \- beans, peas…  
* ½ cup shallots or onions, chopped large   
* 1 Green chilli  
* 1 Tomato  
* 0.5 teaspoon Turmeric  
* A generous pinch asafoetida  
* 1 sprig Curry leaves  
* 2 tablespoon Coriander leaves chopped  
* ½ teaspoon Jaggery  
* Salt

**TO ROAST AND GRIND**

* 1 teaspoon [ghee](https://rakskitchen.net/how-to-make-ghee-from-butter-video-step-by-step-post/)  
* 2 Red chillies, long variety  
* 1 tablespoon Channa dal  
* 1.5 teaspoon Coriander seeds  
* ⅛ teaspoon Fenugreek seeds  
* 4 tablespoon Coconut grated

**Instructions**

* Extract tamarind juice and keep aside.   
* Cook rice and dal in 5.5 cups of water, carrots or squash and 0.5 tsp of turmeric . Instapot \- high pressure for 25 minutes.

* Heat pan and roast to golden brown, the ingredients under ‘To roast and grind table’ with a teaspoon of ghee/oil except coconut.   
* Roast coconut separately.

* Cool and grind to coarse powder or paste with little water and keep a side.

* Heat large pot and temper with the items given under ‘to temper’ table. Add curry leaves followed by onion,slit green chillies and fry till the onions start to turn soften.

   Add the tomatoes and fry till soft.Add the tamarind extract,bring to boil. Add the turmeric,asafoetida and the chopped vegetables.

   When the vegetables lose raw smell, add jaggery, the ground sambar powder and bring to boil.

   Add the ground spices gradually while stirring, as there are chances of forming lumps.Add this to the cooked rice & dal.  Add hot water if it’s too thick. Let the sambar rice be in a loose consistency. After it cools down, it tends to get thick, so adjust consistency with that in mind.  
* Top generously with ghee and coriander leaves.
