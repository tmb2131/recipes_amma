# Sambar with coconut milk

- Tuvar Dal — 0.5+ cup
- Turmeric — 0.5 tsp + 0.5 tsp
- Tamarind — tight large marble size (smaller than golf ball)
- Sambar powder — 1 tbsp
- Salt
- Asafoetida pinch
- Onions or shallots chopped large — 0.75 cup
- Cilantro garnish
- Oil — 3 teaspoons
- Mustard seeds — 0.75 tsp
- Dry red chilli — 1 whole
- Asafoetida
- (RA's readymade sambar mixture in large jar in fridge)
- 1.5 tsp
- Coconut milk 0.5 cup

Soak tamarind in a cup warm or hot water.
Cook tuvar dal and turmeric powder until soft in instant pot.
Squeeze the tamarind well into the water until all (or most) the pulp is removed from the stringy parts.  Discard latter.

Heat 1 tsp of oil.  Fry onions and turmeric for a minute or two.  Add sambar powder and asafoetida and fry on medium low for a further minute.
Add the tamarind liquid.  Bring to a boil and allow to simmer for 5 to 7 minutes or until the mixture has lost its raw smell.

Add cooked dal, salt and a bit of water to bring the sambar to a thick soup consistency.
Allow to simmer for 2 minutes.
(Add RA's sambar mixture now).

Keepin the heat very low, add the  coconut milk slowly while gently stirring.  Turn off heat.  Cover partly and allow to rest.
In a small pot heat oil.  Add mustard seeds, chilli and asafoetida.  Pour over sambar.  Cilantro to garnish.
