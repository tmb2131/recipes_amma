# Asparagus Ricotta Toast (WSJ)

- TOTAL TIME: 25 minutes
- SERVES: 4
- 4 eggs
- 4 tablespoons olive oil, plus more to finish
- 4 (½ — inch thick) slices sour dough bread
- 2 pounds medium asparagus, cut into 3 — inch pieces
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Juiced and finely grated zest of 1 lemon
- 1¼ cup ricotta
- ⅓ cup roughly torn basil leaves, plus more to garnish
- ¼ cup minced chives
- 8 oil — packed anchovy fillets

Fill a medium pot with 4 inches of water and bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Use a slotted spoon to lower eggs into boiling water and cook until soft-boiled, 6½ minutes. Run eggs under cold water until cool enough to handle. Peel eggs and set aside.

While eggs boil, heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Lay in bread and sear until golden and crisp on both sides, 1-2 minutes per side. Set bread aside.

Swirl 1 tablespoon olive oil into skillet and increase heat to high. Working in batches if necessary, to avoid crowding pan, add asparagus and cook until just tender and charred all over, 2-3 minutes. Off heat, season with salt, lemon juice and black pepper.

In a small bowl, combine ricotta and basil, stirring gently to combine. Season with salt, lemon zest and black pepper to taste. Generously smear seasoned ricotta over toasted bread. Heap seared asparagus on top of cheese and sprinkle on chives, more lemon zest and more basil. Top each toast with 2 anchovy fillets and one soft boiled egg. Use a sharp knife to split eggs and so yolk runs out. Season lightly with salt and drizzle olive oil over toasts.
