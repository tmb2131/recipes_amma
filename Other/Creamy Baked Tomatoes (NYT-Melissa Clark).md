# Creamy Baked Tomatoes (NYT-Melissa Clark)

- 2 large tomatoes
- For the Dressing:
- 1 tablespoon chopped olives or capers, a 1 grated or minced garlic clove
- 1 squeeze of lemon or lime juice
- 1 pinch of salt
- 1 or 2 teaspoons of chopped fresh herbs OR ¼ teaspoon dried herbs (basil, oregano)
- 1 — 2 drizzles of olive oil to make it seem like a vinaigrette
- For the Filling:
- 1 cup ricotta cottage cheese or farmers’ cheese)
- ½ cup grated Parmesan
- 1 or 2 teaspoons of chopped fresh herbs OR ¼ teaspoon dried herbs (basil, oregano)
- ½ teaspoon grated lemon zest
- freshly ground black pepper / pinch of red — pepper flakes
- 1 drizzle of olive oil
- salt to taste

Heat the oven to 400°F before you start cutting up the tomatoes. Slice off the tops, just enough to remove the stem end and expose the tomatoes’ insides. Using a spoon (a serrated grapefruit spoon is excellent for this), scoop the tomato guts and seeds into a bowl, leaving the flesh around the perimeter intact so the tomatoes don’t collapse when they cook.

Combine the dressing ingredients in a small bowl, whisking in the olive oil last. Taste and add more salt or lemon, if needed, or maybe some more olives.

In another bowl, mix the filling ingredients. Scoop the filling into the tomatoes, mounding it on top. Put the tomatoes into a baking dish, then bake until the filling is hot and melty and a little browned, 25 to 35 minutes.

Pour the dressing over the tomatoes, and serve with more herbs on top.
