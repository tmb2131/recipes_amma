## Ingredients  
For the onions:  
* 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil  
* 4 anchovy fillets, minced  
* ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes  
* 1 medium onion, peeled, halved and sliced  
* 1 teaspoon (3 grams) Diamond Crystal kosher salt  
* ½ cup plus 1 tablespoon heavy cream  
For the dough:  
* 1½ cups plus 2 tablespoons (226 grams) all-purpose flour  
* 1 tablespoon baking powder  
* 1¼ teaspoons (3.7 grams) Diamond Crystal kosher salt  
* 3 ounces (85 grams) unsalted butter, cut into ½-inch dice and frozen  
* ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper  
* 2½ ounces (71 grams) Gruyère cheese, grated (about ¾ cup), plus more for sprinkling  
* ½ ounce (14 grams) Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated (about 2½ tablespoons)  
## Directions  
1. Start the onions: Combine oil, anchovies and red pepper flakes in a skillet set over medium-low heat. Add onions and 1 teaspoon (3 grams) salt. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally and adjusting heat as necessary, until onions are soft and golden, about 45 minutes. Cool onions, then chop them. Put chopped onions in a bowl, mix in cream, cover with plastic wrap, and chill for at least 15 minutes.  
2. Start the dough: In a food processor, combine flour, baking powder and 1¼ teaspoons (3.7 grams) salt. Pulse to mix. Add frozen butter and process until mixture is the texture of coarse meal, about 15 seconds. Transfer mixture to a bowl and mix in cheeses. Add onion mixture, and use a fork to stir just until onions are evenly distributed.  
3. Finish the biscuits: Preheat oven to 400 degrees and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and form it into a square about 8 by 8 inches. Cut square into quarters with a sharp knife or bench scraper. Stack quarters on top of one another. Use a rolling pin to gently flatten layered dough and then roll it into a square ½ to ¾ inch thick. Trim edges and cut out biscuits, each about 1 inch square. Reroll trimmings and cut additional biscuits. Working in batches if necessary, arrange the biscuits you want to bake in the short term on the prepared baking sheet. Sprinkle them with additional Gruyère. (Freeze remaining biscuits for later use.) Bake biscuits for 7 minutes, rotate pan front to back, and continue baking until tops are golden, about 6 minutes more. Serve warm or at room temperature.
