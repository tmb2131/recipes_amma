1 yellow onion, diced 2 green onions, thinly chopped 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced 2 tsp avocado oil 5 cups low-sodium vegetable broth 425g (15oz) full-fat coconut milk 2 cups pumpkin, cut into 2in cubes (butternut or kabocha squash may be substituted) ½ cup yellow split peas 4 bay leaves 3 sprigs fresh thyme 2 green plantains, peeled and cut into 5cm (2in) pieces 2 Roma tomatoes, coarsely chopped 1 ear of corn, cut lengthwise into 5cm (2in) pieces juice and zest of 2 lemons 1 tsp kosher salt (strict Rastafarians may not use salt, but Feller does) 1 cup okra, cut into 5cm (2in) pieces 1 habanero pepper lime wedges, for garnish 1 bunch fresh coriander, leaves torn, for garnish  
Method  
Step 1 In a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat, place the yellow onion, green onions, garlic and oil. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes, stirring occasionally and taking care not to burn the garlic, until the garlic is golden. Add 1 cup of water and cook for an additional 5 minutes.  
Step 2 Add the broth, coconut milk, pumpkin, split peas, bay leaves and thyme and cook uncovered over medium-high heat for 8 minutes. Next, add the plantains, tomatoes, corn, lemon juice and zest, and salt and simmer over medium heat for 30 minutes.  
Step 3 Add the okra and habanero and simmer over low heat for 15 to 20 minutes; discard the bay leaves and thyme sprigs. To serve, spoon the stew into bowls and garnish each with a wedge of lime and fresh coriander leaves.
