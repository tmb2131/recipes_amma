Savoury gruyère and courgette muffins (BBC)  
  
  
  
* 		225g/8oz [self-raising flour](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/self-raising_flour)  
* 		1 tsp [baking powder](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/baking_powder)  
* 		100g/3½oz [courgette](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/courgette), grated  
* 		salt and freshly ground black pepper  
* 		75g/2¾oz [Gruyère](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/gruyere_cheese), grated (or similar vegetarian cheese, such as emmental)  
* 		175ml/6fl oz full fat [milk](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/milk)  
* 		1 free-range [egg](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/egg), beaten  
* 		75ml/2¾fl oz [olive oil](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/olive_oil), plus extra for greasing  
* 		1 tbsp chopped fresh [rosemary](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/rosemary)  
* 		[butter](https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/butter), to serve  
Recipe tips  
# Method  
1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4. Grease 5 of the holes in a 12-hole muffin tray with olive oil.  
2. Mix the flour, baking powder, courgette, seasoning and 50g/1¾oz of the cheese together in a bowl.  
3. In a separate bowl, whisk the milk, egg and olive oil together until well combined. Add the liquid mixture and rosemary to the dry ingredients and mix until well combined.  
4. Divide the mixture among the muffin holes and bake for 10 minutes in the oven, then sprinkle over the remaining cheese. Bake for a further 10-15, or until risen and golden-brown.  
5. Serve the muffins warm, with butter.
