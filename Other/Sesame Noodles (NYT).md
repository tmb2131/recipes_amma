Sesame Noodles (NYT)  
  
Serves 4  
  
INGREDIENTS  
* **1 **pound noodles, frozen or (preferably) fresh  
* **1 **pound noodles, frozen or (preferably) fresh  
* **2 **tablespoons sesame oil, plus a splash  
* **2 **tablespoons sesame oil, plus a splash  
* **3 ½ **tablespoons soy sauce  
* **3 ½ **tablespoons soy sauce  
* **2 **tablespoons Chinese rice vinegar  
* **2 **tablespoons Chinese rice vinegar  
* **2 **tablespoons Chinese sesame paste  
* **2 **tablespoons Chinese sesame paste  
* **1 **tablespoon smooth peanut butter  
* **1 **tablespoon smooth peanut butter  
* **1 **tablespoon sugar  
* **1 **tablespoon sugar  
* **1 **tablespoon finely grated ginger  
* **1 **tablespoon finely grated ginger  
* **2 **teaspoons minced garlic  
* **2 **teaspoons minced garlic  
* **2 **teaspoons chili-garlic paste, or to taste  
* **2 **teaspoons chili-garlic paste, or to taste  
* 2 tbsp strong black tea  
* Half a cucumber, peeled, seeded, and cut into 1/8-inch by 1/8-inch by 2-inch sticks  
* **¼ **cup chopped roasted peanuts  
* **¼ **cup chopped roasted peanuts  
PREPARATION  
1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add noodles and cook until barely tender, about 5 minutes; they should retain a hint of chewiness. Drain, rinse with cold water, drain again and toss with a splash of sesame oil.  
2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the remaining 2 tablespoons sesame oil, the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame paste, peanut butter, sugar, tea, ginger, garlic and chili-garlic paste.  
3. Pour the sauce over the noodles   
4. Serve with cucumber sticks and roasted peanuts  
5. Serve with cucumber sticks and roasted peanuts
