  
# Broccoli, Lentils and Burrata   
  
## The recipe  
Cook 80g of small dark green lentils, such as Le Puy, in deep, lightly salted boiling water for about 20 minutes or until tender but toothsome. Drain them, add a dash of olive oil and set aside.  
Slice the green florets from the top of a large head of broccoli. Shave any tough bits from the stems with a vegetable peeler, then cut the largest stems into small pieces. Leave any small ones whole.  
Bring a pan of water to the boil, lower in the broccoli stems and let them cook for a couple of minutes then remove, drain and set aside. Peel and finely slice 2 garlic cloves then fry them in 30g of butter in a shallow pan until deep gold, crisp and fragrant. Remove them from the pan and drain on kitchen paper.  
Finely chop and fry a small chilli in the garlic pan, adding a little more butter or oil if necessary, then, when it is soft, add the broccoli florets. Cook for 2 minutes until tender then add the cooked stems and cook for a further 2 minutes until hot.  
Drain a couple of nicely chilled burrata, place them on 2 plates, then spoon over the sizzling broccoli and chilli mixture. Scatter the crisp garlic among them and serve. Enough for 2.  
## The trick  
The warm, crisp garlic and hot chilli-spiked greens are simply gorgeous with the cool, milky burrata. It is worth taking care with frying the garlic, letting it sizzle quietly over a gentle heat, so it browns lightly and evenly as it crisps. Anything more than walnut brown and it will be bitter.  
## The twist  
Slices of mozzarella will work instead of the burrata. I sometimes like to introduce tomatoes – 2 or 3 small ones finely diced – to the broccoli. Use the shaved broccoli and stems, and crisp garlic, over a fried egg or two instead.
