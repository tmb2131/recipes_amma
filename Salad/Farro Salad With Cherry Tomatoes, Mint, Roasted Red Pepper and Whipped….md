* 1 cup whole-milk ricotta  
* 5 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more as needed  
* Kosher salt  
* Salad  
* 2½ cups cooked farro  
* 2 tablespoons Sherry vinegar or red wine vinegar, plus more to taste  
* Chile flakes  
* 1 pint cherry tomatoes  
* 1 medium English cucumber or 2 small Persian cucumbers  
* 1 roasted red pepper cut into ½-inch pieces  
* ½ cup roughly chopped fresh mint  
## Directions  
1. Make whipped ricotta: Add ricotta to a food processor and pulse once or twice. With motor running, add about 2 tablespoons olive oil and ¼ teaspoon salt. Process until creamy, stopping and scraping down sides of bowl as needed. Taste and season with more salt and olive oil as desired.  
2. Assemble salad: Put farro in a large bowl. Drizzle on vinegar and season with a big pinch of salt and chile flakes. Toss thoroughly.  
3. Cut tomatoes in half and add to a separate bowl, along with any juices. Season with a pinch of salt. Peel cucumber, cut in half lengthwise, scrape out seeds and cut into small pieces (if using Persian cucumbers, you can leave seeds in). Add to bowl with tomatoes, along with red pepper and half of mint. Let sit about 5 minutes, until juicy.  
4. Add tomato mixture to farro and toss well. Let sit for about 5 minutes. Season with salt to taste, keeping in mind that the whipped ricotta will make it saltier. Add about 3 tablespoons olive oil, toss and taste again.  
5. Spread a thick swoosh of whipped ricotta on individual plates across a serving platter. Pile farro salad on top and garnish with remaining mint. Serve.
