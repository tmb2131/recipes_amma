##   
Servings: 4-6  
One (15-ounce) can no-salt-added chickpeas, drained, rinsed and patted dry 3 tablespoons plus 2 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided 1/4 teaspoon fine salt, divided, plus more to taste 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, divided, plus more to taste One (12-ounce) tomato, hulled and cut into 1/2-inch dice (2 cups) 1/2 large English cucumber, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch dice (1 1/2 cups) 1 small red bell pepper (4 ounces), stemmed, seeded and cut into 1/2-inch dice (1/2 cup) 1 small yellow or orange bell pepper (4 ounces), stemmed, seeded and cut into 1/2-inch dice (1/2 cup) 1/4 cup diced red onion 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh mint, plus whole leaves for garnish 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar 2 Belgian endives (root ends trimmed), leaves separated 4 ounces (1 cup) crumbled high-quality feta cheese  
##   
 Position a rack in the middle of the oven and preheat to 425 degrees.  
 Spread the chickpeas on a large sheet pan. Drizzle with 2 teaspoons of the oil, season with 1/8 teaspoon each of the salt and pepper, and toss to coat. Roast for 15 minutes, or until the chickpeas have darkened slightly, stirring once halfway through. Transfer to a plate and let cool completely.  
 While the chickpeas are roasting, in a large bowl, combine the tomato, cucumber, bell peppers, red onion, parsley and chopped mint. Drizzle in the remaining 3 tablespoons of oil and the vinegar, along with the remaining 1/8 teaspoon each of salt and pepper. Gently toss to incorporate. Taste, and season with more salt and pepper, as needed.  
 Line a serving platter with the endive leaves. Spoon the chopped vegetable mixture over them. Scatter the roasted chickpeas and the feta on top, then garnish with mint leaves.
