# Mujadara with spiced yogurt & yogurt cucumber salad (WSJ)

- Serves 4 — 6
- 7½ cups water
- 1 cup Beluga lentils
- 1 sprig fresh thyme
- 1 yellow onion, halved, plus 1½ onions, thinly sliced
- Kosher salt
- 4 tablespoons butter
- 1 cup fideo noodles
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon baharat spice
- 2 cups jasmine rice, rinsed
- ¼ cup olive oil
- 1 cup Greek yogurt
- 3 cloves garlic, grated
- 3 tablespoons lemon juice
- 2 — 3 Persian cucumbers, sliced in half moons
- 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
- ½ red onion, thinly sliced
- 10 mint leaves
- 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses
- ½ tablespoon sumac
- ½ tablespoon za’atar

In a medium pot, combine 3 cups water with the lentils, thyme and a half onion. Bring to boil, then simmer until tender, 15-20 minutes. Drain, discard onion and season lentils with salt. Spread lentils on a baking sheet to cool.

Meanwhile, finely dice remaining onion half. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add noodles. Season with cumin and baharat. Sauté until golden, then stir in diced onion. Season with salt and cook until onion is translucent. Add 4½ cups water and a pinch salt. Bring to a simmer, stir in rice and reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer until rice is fluffy, about 15 minutes. Let rice cool slightly, at least 5 minutes, before gently folding in cooked lentils.

Meanwhile, combine oil and sliced onions in a medium pan over medium heat. Sauté until caramelized, about 10 minutes. Season with salt.

In a small bowl, combine yogurt, garlic, 2 tablespoons lemon juice and 1½ teaspoons salt. In another bowl, combine cucumbers, tomatoes, red onion, mint, pomegranate molasses, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, sumac and za’atar. Toss.
