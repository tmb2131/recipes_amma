# Butter Bean and Mushroom Soupy Stew (WSJ)

- Serves 4
- ## Ingredients
- 1½ cups dried butter (lima) beans
- 3 cloves garlic
- 2 fresh bay leaves
- 2 white onions, 1 halved
- 1 ounce dried mushrooms
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 14 ounces thin — skinned potatoes (such as Yukon Gold), scrubbed but unpeeled, left whole if small or chopped into chunks if large
- Handful of kale, tough stalks removed, roughly chopped
- 3½ ounces cooked chestnuts, roughly chopped (optional)
- 1 medium kabocha, delica or butternut squash
- Vegetable oil
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus more to serve
- ¼ cup fresh marjoram, oregano, thyme or tarragon leaves, chopped, plus more to serve
- Good-quality wine vinegar or kraut brine
- Sour cream or creme fraiche

1. <br />

   ***

   Soak beans overnight in a large bowl covered with 8½ cups cold water. Transfer beans to a large pot and add water to cover by 2 inches. Add garlic, bay leaves, halved onion, a couple of the dried mushrooms and a few pinches of salt. Cover with parchment paper so everything stays under water.
2. <br />
3. Simmer over low heat until beans are soft and creamy but still keep their shape, 1-1½ hours. Do not drain (there should be a fair amount of bean water). Fish out and compost onion halves.
4. <br />
5. Add potatoes and a generous pinch of salt. Cook until potatoes are cooked through and crushable, 10-15 minutes. Taste broth for salt: Make sure it is properly seasoned, or the end dish will taste underwhelming. Add kale and chestnuts, if using, and switch off heat. Preheat oven to 475 degrees.
6. <br />
7. Meanwhile, wash and trim squash, cut it in half, scoop out seeds and slice into 1-inch wedges. Place on a parchment lined baking sheet and sprinkle with a little vegetable oil and salt. Bake until a little charred and cooked through but still firm, about 25 minutes.
8. <br />
9. Chop remaining onion. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a small frying pan and sweat onion with a pinch of salt over low heat, 10 minutes.
10. <br />
11. Use a mortar and pestle or food processor to pound remaining dried mushrooms into a rough powder. (You don’t want a fine powder, so don’t overdo it.) Add crushed mushrooms to pan with onions and cook, stirring, until sticky, 2-4 minutes. If the pan looks dry, add a few tablespoons of bean-cooking water. Season generously with pepper. Add herbs to bean pot.
12. <br />
13. Stir onion-mushroom mixture into beans, taste for seasoning, and reheat. Add squash and another pinch of chopped herbs on top. Serve with a drizzle of olive oil, a splash of vinegar or brine, sour cream or crème fraîche, and plenty of crusty bread.
